Project Name and Classical Source

The project name "Lejiaohao" (乐郊号) follows the pinyin spelling of "乐郊号". Its core phrase, "Lejiao" (乐郊), comes from The Classic of Poetry (《诗经》), one of the major classics of early Chinese and world literature. In "Large Rat" (Shuoshu, 《诗经·硕鼠》):

硕鼠硕鼠，无食我苗！三岁贯女，莫我肯劳。逝将去女，适彼乐郊。乐郊乐郊，谁之永号？

In classical Chinese, "乐郊", close in sense to "乐土", can be read as a joyful and abundant land: a humane place of sustenance, dignity, and release from oppression. The Lejiaohao project carries this classical prospect into future studies, asking what kind of better world may be possible under physical constraints.

The appended "hao" ("号") is a later project-name element. In the current project, Lejiaohao refers to both a future-oriented archive and a distributed civilizational megastructure concept.
